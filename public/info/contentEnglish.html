Halakhic conciderations

We follow accepted halakhic principles for approval and inspection of food-production facilities. In some cases, there exist different approach among rabbis. In these cases we have follow these decisions/halackic rulings:

Milk

We accept chalav akum based on Rabbi Mosher Feinstein’s ruling.

Cheese

Only cheeses produced with a mashgiach present at the production is kosher, even if the rennet in a cheese is fully vegetarian.

Bread

We accept bread that is not Pat Israel, i.e. bread that is baked by non-Jews.

E 120

We accept the additive E 120 based on halackic rulings of recocknised Rabbinical authorities (some rabbis don’t approve this additive).

Symbols used in the list

  • M = Dairy
  • M* = The products doesn’t contain milk but is produces in a production line also used for dairy products. Should not be eaten together with meat/meat-products. No waiting after meat-based meal is required.
  • P = Parve

A guide for tourists who wants a kosher stay in Norway

The products that appear in this list are available in most convenience stores and supermarkets. Most of these products are not marked as kosher. You are dependent on the list to be able to identify the kosher-approved products.

Kosher meat, cheese and other special products are available in the kosher-shop in The Jewish Community, Bergstien 13, Oslo. However, the kosher-shop has limited opening hours. Presently the shop is open Wednesdays from 15.00 to 19.00. During summer and other vacation seasons, opening hours can be further limited.

If you cook your own food, there are many ways to live kosher. You can buy kosher fish in virtually all supermarkets. Disposable grills are widely available, though you should always check if it permitted to use grills in parks or in other public areas. If you hire a cabin, there will very often be a small kitchen in the cabin. Such a kitchen is obviously not kosher. Still, if you bring your own pot or frying pan, such equipment can be used on the Stovetop. Disposable plates and cutlery are available in all supermarkets.

What can you eat at the breakfast buffet that most hotels in Norway serve? All not-mixed fruit juices i.e. pressed fresh fruits (for example orange-juice or apple-juice) are kosher. Coffee and tea are kosher. Swedish crackers (“knekkebrød”) from WASA are kosher and you find them in the breakfast buffet in most Norwegian hotels. Cereals form Kellogg’s are also kosher. Milk, butter, honey and smoked salmon are all kosher. All fresh vegetables besides onion can also be eaten. The hotels plates and cutlery can only be used for cold food. Boiled eggs can only be eaten if they are cooked in a steamer in a pot that is exclusively used for this purpose. Most Norwegian hotels boil eggs this way.
